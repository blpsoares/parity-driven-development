# Getting Support

Thanks for using Parity-Driven Development (PDD). Here is where to get help.

## Read the docs first

Most questions are answered in the documentation:

- [Documentation hub](./docs/README.md) — concepts, guides, reference, and install.
- [Quickstart](./QUICKSTART.md) — from zero to your first `verified` finding, one command at a time, with a real worked example.

## Help inside the terminal

You do not have to leave your workflow to get oriented:

- **`pdd` dashboard → Legend tab** — plain-language explanations of coverage %, confidence tiers, and the full pipeline, plus the same command reference in-terminal. Run `pdd` (or `pdd tui`) and open the **Legend** tab.
- **`/audit-status`** — an in-chat summary of parity-coverage %, confidence distribution, and active tasks.

## Check what changed recently

If something behaves differently than the docs describe, or you just upgraded, check the [CHANGELOG](./CHANGELOG.md) — it follows Keep a Changelog and records every notable change.

## Open a GitHub issue

For bugs, questions, or feature ideas, open an issue on the tracker:

- [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/blpsoares/parity-driven-development/issues)

When reporting a bug, include your agent/harness (Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, Copilot, or Gemini), your runtime (Node or Bun), the command you ran, and what you expected versus what happened. Concrete, observable facts ("shows 3 items, should show 5") help far more than "it's broken."
